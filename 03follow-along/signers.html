Who Were the Signers

Famous Signers

Some of the most famous signers of the Declaration of Independence include:

Thomas Jefferson (Virginia)
Principal author of the Declaration and later the 3rd President of the United States.
John Adams (Massachusetts)
Strong advocate for independence, member of the drafting committee, later the 2nd President of the United States.
Benjamin Franklin (Pennsylvania)
Elder statesman, respected scientist, diplomat, and key figure in gaining French support for the Revolution.
John Hancock (Massachusetts)
President of the Continental Congress; known for his large, bold signature, making his name synonymous with "signature" in American culture.
Samuel Adams (Massachusetts)
Prominent revolutionary leader, organizer of the Boston Tea Party, and influential in rallying public support for independence.
Charles Carroll (Maryland)
The only Catholic signer, and the last surviving signer (he died in 1832).
Roger Sherman (Connecticut)
Only person to sign the Continental Association, the Declaration, the Articles of Confederation, and the U.S. Constitution.
Robert Morris (Pennsylvania)
Known as the “Financier of the Revolution,” he played a critical role in securing funding for the Continental Army.

Each of these individuals played a significant role in the founding of the United States and is remembered for their courage in signing the declaration, an act considered treasonous by the British crown.
